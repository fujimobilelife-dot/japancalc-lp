FAQ

Does JapanCalc work offline?

Yes. All core features work offline. Currency rates are cached from your last online session. Connect to Wi-Fi for the most current rates.

Where do the exchange rates come from?

Rates are fetched from a public exchange rate API and updated automatically when the app is open and connected to the internet.

Does JapanCalc collect my personal data?

No. JapanCalc does not collect, store, or transmit any personally identifiable information. All trip data stays on your device. See our Privacy Policy.

Is this a subscription? Can I get a refund?

JapanCalc is a one-time purchase of $2.99 — no subscriptions, no renewals. Refunds are handled by Google Play's standard policy (within 48 hours of purchase).

What is an IC card? Do I need one?

IC cards (Suica, PASMO) are rechargeable transit cards used on trains and buses across Japan. Highly recommended — faster and cheaper than buying individual tickets. JapanCalc's HOW TO RIDE tab explains how to get and use one.

How does tax-free shopping work in Japan?

Visitors can claim a 10% consumption tax refund on purchases over ¥5,000 at participating stores. You'll need your passport. JapanCalc's TAX FREE tab helps you check eligibility instantly.

The app isn't working. What should I do?

Try force-closing and reopening the app. If the issue persists, use the contact form below and describe the problem — we'll get back to you as soon as possible.
